# Services commercial redesign

## Objective

Redesign the localized services hub and every service detail page so they match the restrained professional system already used by the home, about, projects, and blog pages while becoming easier to scan and more effective at generating qualified enquiries.

The redesign must make the offer feel accessible without positioning José Miguel as a low-cost provider. It will do this by presenting three ways to engage for each service: a small concrete intervention, a complete project, and ongoing monthly support. The existing free first assessment remains the primary conversion promise.

## Audience and commercial position

The primary audiences are:

- Small businesses that need a clearer website, less manual work, or dependable maintenance.
- Independent professionals and consultants who need a credible online presence and direct technical support.
- Founders and small product teams that need a focused first version, integration, automation, or backend intervention.
- Technical clients that need implementation capacity, a second opinion, or ongoing maintenance.

The offer should communicate direct access to an experienced full-stack developer, pragmatic scope, maintainable delivery, and visible entry prices. It must not compete by claiming that AI makes professional work almost free. AI may reduce implementation time, but analysis, integration, testing, deployment, communication, and accountability remain part of the service.

## Research conclusions

The existing catalog already contains seven core services and eighteen generated offering pages. The redesign should improve hierarchy rather than create many more disconnected pages.

DataForSEO research for Spain supports the four selected commercial areas. The strongest relevant signals include approximately 1,600 monthly searches for `consultoría informática`, 1,000 for `diseño web wordpress`, 880 for `mantenimiento web`, 720 for `automatización de procesos`, 590 for `mantenimiento wordpress`, 390 for `software a medida`, 320 for `ia para empresas`, and 260 for `automatización con ia`.

The clearest catalog gap is general maintenance and technical support. `Auditoría web` and `SEO técnico` are better treated as small entry interventions inside web and maintenance than as separate top-level business areas.

## Approved commercial architecture

The public hub will present four primary areas. Each area shows an icon, a short outcome-oriented description, three featured services, one visible entry price, and a link to explore the area.

### 1. Web and conversion

Featured services:

- Business website.
- Website redesign.
- Landing pages.

Supporting offers such as WordPress design, WooCommerce, migration, optimization, and technical web audits remain available in the compact directory and their existing detail routes.

### 2. Custom software

Featured services:

- Custom web application.
- Internal tools.
- API integrations.

Supporting offers include IT project management, IT advisory, technology second opinions, and related backend work.

### 3. Automation and applied AI

Featured services:

- Workflow automation.
- AI automation.
- Chatbots and assistants.

Supporting offers include AI for companies, AI agents, integrations, and practical AI training.

### 4. Maintenance and technical support

This is a new core service, represented by the typed slug `maintenance-support` and equivalent localized public routes and copy.

Featured services:

- Website and WordPress maintenance.
- Technical audit and optimization.
- Application and backend maintenance.

The service covers corrective interventions, preventive work, performance and stability improvements, dependency and security updates, deployment support, and recurring technical assistance. It does not promise round-the-clock incident response unless a future agreement explicitly includes it.

## Pricing model

Every public service detail page must present three meaningful engagement options. They are scope levels, not artificial bundles of hours.

1. `intervention`: a small, concrete deliverable with a clear boundary.
2. `project`: a complete outcome and the most common option.
3. `support`: recurring monthly maintenance, improvement, or advisory capacity.

The hub shows only an entry price for each featured service or area. The detail page shows all three options with inclusions, exclusions, price, billing unit, and CTA.

Approved area-level starting points are:

| Area | One-off intervention | Complete project | Monthly support |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Web and conversion | From EUR 120 | From EUR 590 | From EUR 69/month |
| Custom software | From EUR 250 | From EUR 1,200 | From EUR 149/month |
| Automation and applied AI | From EUR 180 | From EUR 650 | From EUR 99/month |
| Maintenance and technical support | From EUR 90 | From EUR 290 | From EUR 69/month |

Prices are displayed as `Desde X € + IVA` in Spanish and `From €X + VAT` in English. The final quote depends on scope, integrations, current system condition, data quality, risk, and delivery requirements.

These figures are area baselines, not universal values copied across all pages. Every generated offering must have an explicit price configuration and deliverables appropriate to that offering. No offering may silently inherit an unrelated service's copy or prices.

## Services hub information architecture

The localized services hub will use this order:

1. Commercial hero.
2. Four primary service areas.
3. Three engagement models.
4. Selected proof and projects.
5. Four-step delivery process.
6. Compact full-service directory.
7. FAQ.
8. Shared conversion CTA.

### Hero

The hero uses a short outcome-led headline, one supporting paragraph, the free first assessment as an eyebrow or trust signal, a coral primary CTA, and a neutral secondary link to selected projects. It must avoid the current long generic introduction.

The hero may mention that work can start with a small intervention, but it must not use the lowest price as the dominant headline. Prices belong to the service cards where their scope has context.

### Primary area grid

The approved direction is the `A · Escaparate de soluciones` concept: a two-by-two desktop grid of large service-area cards. Each card contains:

- A distinctive line icon.
- Area title and one outcome-oriented sentence.
- Three featured service links.
- An entry-price label.
- A clear secondary action to explore the area or its primary service.

On mobile, the four cards stack vertically. They do not use a carousel because hiding services would make comparison harder.

### Engagement models

A concise section explains the difference between an intervention, a complete project, and monthly support. It clarifies what a buyer can start with without repeating all prices.

### Proof

Show selected project or result cards related to web, software, automation, and backend work. Reuse the project-card visual language and link to the relevant case study. Maintenance may use a credible technical proof point or related article when no dedicated case study exists.

### Compact directory

All existing offering routes remain discoverable. Use four native `details` groups or an equally accessible compact disclosure pattern, one per area. Each group contains short service links and no duplicate long descriptions. This preserves internal linking and discovery without returning to a wall of cards.

### Content reduction

The current separate sections for definition, advantages, recommended audience, use cases, proof points, and supporting content overlap heavily. Their strongest content should be rewritten into the hero, area cards, engagement model, proof, FAQ, and CTA. The redesign must be shorter and more visual while preserving specific search intent.

## Service detail information architecture

Every localized service route uses the same reusable template in this order:

1. Split service hero.
2. Trust strip.
3. Problem and expected result.
4. Three pricing options.
5. Included and excluded scope.
6. Service-specific process.
7. Related proof or article.
8. Related services.
9. FAQ.
10. Shared conversion CTA.

### Detail hero

The left side contains the service icon, eyebrow, `h1`, concise description, and main CTA. The right side contains a compact commercial summary with the entry price, expected starting timeline when known, and a link to compare the three engagement options.

The existing WhatsApp path remains available as a secondary action. `View all services` moves out of the main CTA row and becomes a quieter back link or breadcrumb action.

### Trust strip

Use three concise trust signals:

- Free first assessment.
- Direct communication with the person doing the work.
- Clear ownership of code, accounts, and handoff.

### Pricing options

Render three comparable cards. `Project` is visually marked as `Más habitual` / `Most common` using position, border, and a small label rather than an unrelated bright color. Each option includes:

- Label and short explanation.
- Numeric starting price in EUR.
- `one-off` or `per month` unit.
- Three to five concrete deliverables.
- A short boundary or exclusion.
- CTA carrying service slug and pricing key to contact.

The options stack vertically on mobile. They do not use a carousel.

### Contact flow

Pricing CTAs link to the existing localized contact flow with `service` and `scope` query parameters. The form or modal uses those values to show and submit the selected service and engagement model. There is no checkout, deposit, or automatic purchase flow.

## Visual system

The services pages must share the established system used across the rest of the site:

- Maximum content width of 88rem and reduced large-screen margins.
- Existing technical grid background and light-first default theme.
- Existing navy, coral, surface, muted-text, border, and shadow tokens.
- Existing card radius and restrained shadow treatment used by project and blog cards.
- Coral `button-action` for primary conversion actions and neutral `button-secondary` for secondary actions.
- Line icons from the existing `astro-icon` system, not emoji, placed in soft coral icon containers.
- Strong editorial display typography and shorter paragraphs.
- Dark-mode compatibility with no new hard-coded light-only surfaces.

Icons must be mapped by area and service in typed data so the same service uses the same visual symbol on the hub, detail page, related-service cards, and directory where applicable.

## Data and component architecture

Extend the service data contract to include:

- `area`: one of the four approved service areas.
- `icon`: a valid icon identifier.
- `featured`: whether the service appears in the primary hub grid.
- `pricingOptions`: exactly three localized options keyed by `intervention`, `project`, and `support`.
- Optional service-specific timeline and exclusion copy.

Store numeric price values separately from localized display labels so structured data can use EUR amounts without parsing strings. Each pricing option contains its localized label, description, deliverables, boundary, numeric starting price, billing unit, and CTA label.

Generated offering pages must resolve an explicit pricing record by `translationKey`. A missing record is a build-time validation failure. The existing fallback that lets an offering inherit all pricing from a canonical service must be removed.

Create focused reusable components for:

- Service area card.
- Service icon badge.
- Engagement-model summary.
- Pricing option card and pricing grid.
- Service trust strip.
- Service proof card.
- Compact service directory.

Keep route files responsible for composition and SEO data, not repeated card markup.

## Localization

All visible public copy and new service data must exist in Spanish and English with equivalent intent rather than literal translation. This includes:

- New maintenance service and related offerings.
- Area titles and descriptions.
- Pricing labels, units, deliverables, boundaries, tax notes, and CTAs.
- Trust-strip copy.
- Directory labels.
- FAQ additions.
- Contact form selected-service and selected-scope labels.

Localized alternate paths must continue to pair equivalent service offerings.

## SEO and AI discovery

The redesign preserves all existing useful service routes and internal links. It also adds the new maintenance service to:

- Service route generation.
- Localized service copy.
- Hub structured data.
- Service structured data.
- `public/llms.txt`.
- Relevant internal links from the hub, home, blog posts, and related services where natural.

Extend service structured data with valid `Offer` or `OfferCatalog` information for the three numeric starting prices, using `EUR`, the localized service URL, and the provider already present in the schema builders. Do not claim a fixed final price when the visible copy says `from`.

The hub remains specific to commercial service intent. It should not create new top-level pages solely because a keyword has volume. `Auditoría web` and `SEO técnico` are represented as concrete interventions and internal links within the appropriate area.

## Accessibility and responsive behavior

- Preserve one descriptive `h1` per page and logical heading order.
- Decorative icons use `aria-hidden="true"`; meaningful icon-only controls require labels.
- Pricing cards remain understandable without color and follow a logical DOM order.
- Native disclosures remain keyboard accessible.
- Buttons and links preserve visible focus styles and minimum touch target sizes.
- Pricing comparison never relies on horizontal scrolling.
- Service-area cards stack on mobile without hiding content in a carousel.
- Long localized titles and price labels must wrap without overflow.
- Light and dark themes must meet readable contrast requirements.

## Validation

Add or extend focused verification so the build fails when:

- A service or offering lacks an icon or area.
- A service or offering lacks one of the three pricing keys.
- A price is missing, non-numeric, or has no billing unit.
- Spanish and English offering records cannot be paired.
- The maintenance service or its alternate path is missing.
- A generated public service route is missing.
- Hub and detail pages omit required reusable components or conversion markers.

Before completion, run the repository's complete validation set: formatting, Astro checks, lint, AI/SEO verification, focused service verification, and production build. Manually inspect Spanish and English hub/detail routes at mobile and desktop widths in light and dark themes. Verify the contact flow with each pricing key and confirm that no existing localized service URL regresses.

## Out of scope

- Online checkout or payment collection.
- Hourly-rate tables.
- Twenty-four-hour support guarantees.
- A client portal or subscription-management system.
- New service areas beyond the approved four.
- Removing existing useful routes purely to simplify the visual hub.
